The Cruise Industry: Pros and Cons for Urban Tourism in Copenhagen

November 05, 2018 • 0 |

Copenhagen is considered the leader in ‘turnaround’ cruises in North Europe and one of the most used departure ports for Baltic Sea journeys according to VisitCopenhagen. Last year, Copenhagen received a record amount of 875.000 cruise visitors from 150 nationalities, by offering an infrastructure development namely by three cruise quays: Nordre Toldbold, Langelinie Pier and Ocean Quay.

Close Lightbox

According to the Cruise Market Watch, the cruise ship industry has grown in the last year with 3,3%. Due to its popularity, passengers demand, facilities onboard, affordable prices and shorter time of the cruises, this industry has increased its market by building bigger and faster vessels for a larger number of consumers. However, the majority of people concentrated in one place for a short period of time impacts destinations in economical, socio-cultural and environmental ways. The knowledge presented in this blog could help to plan and deal with tourist flows to develop adequate infrastructural plans for a better urban tourism in Copenhagen.

The Piers

By looking at reviews from the online cruise review site CruiseCritic.co.uk we find data on 121 reviews from June - October 2017. In this period, we see on the table below, that these three piers were visited by 28 different cruise ships, and that Ocean Quay is the most used pier. This makes sense as this is the biggest and newest pier of the three with multiple terminals. In relation to infrastructure, it is then important to focus on Ocean Quay to accommodate all the cruise ship passengers going ashore.

Out of the 28 cruise ships we see that the Norwegian Getaway cruise is the most used based on the reviews (figure 1+2). Again, this is also the biggest ship hosting up to 3.969 passengers, and so it makes sense that there is a bigger amount of reviews from this ship. On the other hand, we have Azamara and Le Boreal cruises dock in Toldbod since this is much smaller and only can contain cruise ships with a capacity of 600+ passengers. Months and Reviews

If we look at the monthly distribution of the cruise reviews (figure 3), we see that the most reviews come from the cruises that took place in June and August. It appears that passengers attending cruises in July either do not write as many reviews as those in June and August or there simply isn’t as many people docking in Copenhagen in July. All three of these months are considered the high seasons of tourism, making this the time to be aware of the economic and socio-cultural implications that these cruise ships can cause such as traffic issues, pickpockets and longer ques in general

Independent or Shore Excursion

So when passengers come ashore in Copenhagen is it to participate in a planned excursion or to do some independent urban exploration? According to the reviews, there is an overwhelming amount of people going on an independent excursion (figure 4+5). As shown in figure 5, 76,86% of the passengers go on an independent excursion, which means, that when cruise ships come to Copenhagen passengers want to explore the city on their own, without any agencies planning the trip. Especially the Toldbod pier having 100% of its reviews (figure 6) going on an independent excursion makes sense since this is the closest to the center of Copenhagen and many tourist attractions.

Even though Oceankaj is the pier located furthest away from the center, we also see a majority of the reviews from here going on independent trips. This might be due to the pricing of the excursions, nice weather or the lack of information about the distance to the center leading them to go on an independent excursion. Either way is it important knowledge for the different agencies to cater to the tourists need as well as the locals' day-to-day life.

Popular Attractions
Close Lightbox

So, what do people focus on in the reviews? What do they do in the city? In a word cloud (figure 9) based on both the negative and positive reviews from CruiseCritic, focused on their Copenhagen experience, which is why a word like Copenhagen is the most used. We then get a culmination of what the reviews focus on when visiting the city of Copenhagen. The focus is then on two parts: means of transportation and tourist attractions. The hop on/off-busses seems to be a popular choice together with walking, which all makes sense knowing that most people go on independent trips. Furthermore, we see the most popular attractions: The Little Mermaid, Tivoli, Canal Boat, Gardens, museums, Nyhavn, and Rosenborg. From this, we also get an idea of the sentiments, when words like expensive but also beautiful are quite prominent.

In this blog raises attention to the fact that these cruise ships and passengers are a massive economic resource, that needs to be catered to. Especially Oceankaj as the biggest but also furthest away with a lot of passengers going on independent excursions. Different agencies need to consider the transport problems and options from this area to exploit the economic possibilities and make the attractions more accessible. Even if this blog only touches on the surface of the cruise ship environment it is prominent to understand and convey common issues and intel of the tourist business.
